My Reflection Week 3's Content Connecting With UI and UX-

After reading Mailloux's article, “Convention and Context” and attending the lecture discussion. In UI design, conventions are used to create a sense of familiarity and relief for the user's navigation. By placing creative interface elements that are engaging and easy to use. Mailloux's article reminds us that conventions must be understood in the context they are being used. Thus, appropriate UI conventions should be used to design websites. Mailloux's emphasis on interactivity is related to UX. Interactivity is a key aspect to the modern digital environment which comes with its pros and cons. Users engage with the UI which in turn relates to the User's experience as an outcome. Interactivity in UI should be designed to support and enhance the user's goals and expectations. This can also be related to the lecture discussion on the significance of interaction design in creating effective websites and how important creating a user-friendly interface associate to a positive user experience.

Interactions and Conventions Pros and Cons-

Pros including responsiveness and feedback. Users expect a quick response time and feedback when interacting with digital products, by ensuring your website responds quickly and efficiently with visual cues such as colour, text based messages and shapes is critical for user input. Another would be intuitive design as the interface should be easy to operate, and the layout should be clear, simple and logical, making it easy for users to navigate through ones website. One more being personalization, where users have personalized experiences through the content they are being recommended in accordance to user behaviour and emotional response.

Cons involving poor design decisions which could lead to user becoming frustrated and confused with inconsistent navigation and lack of feedback resulting in a poor user experience. Pop-ups and advertisements can be intrusive and distracting, disrupting the user experience and can lead to user emotional behaviour such as annoyance, irritation and even anger.
